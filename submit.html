Malaysia Water Consumption for Domestic/Non-Domestic Purpose by State

"How have water consumption of States in Malaysia changed over time?"

In this chart, we see that Malaysia's water consumption for purposes has evolved significantly from 2003 to 2022. For instance, in 2003, the overall domestic water consumption was relatively low, but as the population grew and urbanization increased, we observed a steady rise in water usage. By 2010, domestic water consumption had increased substantially, reflecting a growing demand for household water supply. The trend continued, and by 2022, we see that water consumption per capita has reached new heights, emphasizing the importance of sustainable water management practices in addressing this growing demand.

Malaysia Water Production

From 2000 to 2022, Malaysia's annual water production steadily increased, driven by population growth, industrial demands, and climate conditions. The government has invested in improving water management and infrastructure to ensure sustainable supply and quality. However, challenges such as water pollution and equitable access remain.

Malaysia Water Production by State

Water production in Malaysia showed a general increase from 2000 to 2022. Notably, Selangor state exhibited a significant surge in water supply compared to other states. Sarawak also demonstrated a gradual increase, while Kedah experienced a considerable rise as well.

Scatter Plot between Water Access and Water Production

Overall data indicates that most main states in Malaysia had water access percentages close to 100% since 2000. Over time, water production has increased. The most notable trend is the rise in water production alongside the increase in access in Kelantan. Additionally, both Sabah and Sarawak exhibit similar patterns of growth in water production.
